About  Map501
Map501 was created as a final capstone project by Bradley Andrick in completion of his M.S. at the University of Wisconsin–Madison's award-winning Master’s of Science in Cartography/GIS program.

The intention was to create a curated hub/website for organizing and presenting GIS basics and mapping options that are available to low or no-budget nonprofit organizations. These groups are generally limited in knowledge about what options they have and where to begin. In order to attain this knowledge, they either have to spend significant time with a consultant (something that is not always available when volunteering) or google search their way through links to various sites/options and hope that they can put the pieces together in their limited free time. This is where a Knowledge Hub for Non-profit Mapping would come in.

The idea is simple: create a curated, well designed interface that illustrates the general options available to non-profits and how mapping tools may fit their needs or particular use case.
